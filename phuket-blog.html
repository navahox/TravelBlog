Phuket: Paradise on Earth

Discover the Beauty of Phuket

1. Patong Beach

Patong Beach is the most famous beach in Phuket, known for its vibrant nightlife and bustling atmosphere. The beach offers a wide range of water activities, from jet skiing to parasailing. It's a perfect spot for those looking to enjoy sunbathing, beach sports, and the lively ambiance of Phuket's main tourist hub.

Entrance Fee: Free

Dress Code: Beachwear

How to get there: Patong Beach is easily accessible by taxi or tuk-tuk from most parts of the island. It's about a 30-minute drive from Phuket Town and a 45-minute drive from Phuket International Airport. Public buses also run regularly from the town to the beach.

2. Big Buddha

The Big Buddha is one of Phuket's most revered landmarks. Sitting atop Nakkerd Hill, this 45-meter tall statue offers panoramic views of the island. The site is peaceful and provides a spiritual experience, attracting visitors seeking tranquility and breathtaking scenery.

Entrance Fee: Free (Donations appreciated)

Dress Code: Modest attire is required.

How to get there: The Big Buddha is located between Chalong and Kata and can be reached by car or motorbike. The road leading up to the hill is well-signposted. It's a 6 km drive from Chalong Circle, and parking is available at the site.

View on Google Maps

3. Phang Nga Bay

Phang Nga Bay is famous for its limestone karsts that jut vertically out of the emerald-green water. A boat tour through the bay is a must-do, allowing you to explore hidden caves, lagoons, and picturesque islands like James Bond Island and Koh Panyee. It's a stunning natural wonder that offers a sense of adventure and awe.

Entrance Fee: Varies by tour

Dress Code: Casual

How to get there: Tours to Phang Nga Bay can be booked from most hotels and travel agencies in Phuket. The bay is accessible by boat from several piers, including Ao Por Pier and Royal Phuket Marina. It's advisable to join a guided tour for the best experience.
