Bangkok: The City That Never Sleeps

Explore the Wonders of Bangkok

1. Wat Arun (Temple of Dawn)

Wat Arun is known for its stunning riverside location and iconic spires. The temple is beautifully adorned with colorful porcelain and seashells, making it a breathtaking sight, especially at sunrise and sunset. Climbing the steep steps to the top offers a panoramic view of the Chao Phraya River and the surrounding area, making it a fun and rewarding experience.

Entrance Fee: 100 THB

Dress Code: Respectful attire is recommended.

How to get there: To reach Wat Arun, take the BTS Skytrain to Saphan Taksin station. From there, walk to the Sathorn Pier and catch a Chao Phraya Express Boat. Disembark at Tha Tien Pier and take a short ferry ride across the river directly to Wat Arun. The journey offers scenic views of the river and is a pleasant way to travel.

2. Icon Siam

Icon Siam is a luxurious shopping complex that offers more than just shopping. It features stunning architecture and a beautiful riverside location. Inside, you'll find a mesmerizing indoor floating market, exquisite art installations, and a wide variety of dining options. It's a fun place to explore with something for everyone, from high-end fashion to unique cultural experiences.

Entrance Fee: Free

Dress Code: Casual

How to get there: Start your journey by taking the BTS Skytrain to Saphan Taksin station. From there, head to the Sathorn Pier and board the free Icon Siam shuttle boat. The boat ride is not only convenient but also offers a scenic view of the Chao Phraya River, adding to the excitement of your visit.

View on Google Maps

3. Asiatique The Riverfront

Asiatique The Riverfront combines a night bazaar with a mall, offering a unique shopping and entertainment experience. The beautifully restored warehouses house over 1,500 boutiques and 40 restaurants. The atmosphere is lively and fun, with street performances and a giant Ferris wheel offering stunning views of the city. It's a perfect place for an evening stroll by the river.

Entrance Fee: Free

Dress Code: Casual

How to get there: Asiatique is easily accessible by taking the BTS Skytrain to Saphan Taksin station. From there, you can catch a free shuttle boat that takes you directly to the pier at Asiatique. The boat ride is enjoyable and provides a great view of the city skyline.
