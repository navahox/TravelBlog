Ayutthaya: A Journey Through Time

Explore the Ancient Capital of Siam

1. Ayutthaya Historical Park

The Ayutthaya Historical Park is a UNESCO World Heritage Site, home to the ruins of the ancient city of Ayutthaya. Explore the magnificent temples and palaces that tell the story of Thailand's rich history and cultural heritage.

Entrance Fee: 50 THB

Dress Code: Respectful attire recommended

How to get there: Ayutthaya is located about 80 km north of Bangkok. You can reach Ayutthaya by train from Hua Lamphong Station in Bangkok, which takes about 1.5 to 2 hours. Alternatively, you can take a bus or minivan from Mo Chit Bus Terminal, or hire a taxi for a more direct route.

View on Google Maps

2. Wat Mahathat

Wat Mahathat is famous for the Buddha head entwined in tree roots. This iconic site offers a glimpse into the spiritual and architectural grandeur of ancient Ayutthaya.

Entrance Fee: 50 THB

Dress Code: Respectful attire required

How to get there: Wat Mahathat is located within the Ayutthaya Historical Park, easily accessible by bicycle or tuk-tuk from the city center.

View on Google Maps

3. Wat Chaiwatthanaram

Wat Chaiwatthanaram is one of Ayutthaya's most impressive temple complexes, featuring a large central prang surrounded by smaller stupas. It offers stunning views, especially at sunset.

Entrance Fee: 50 THB

Dress Code: Respectful attire required

How to get there: Located on the west bank of the Chao Phraya River, Wat Chaiwatthanaram can be reached by bicycle or tuk-tuk from the city center. It's about a 15-minute ride from the Ayutthaya Historical Park.
